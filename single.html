What comes NeXT

AUTHOR
Rodolfo
DATE
August 08, 2019
CATEGORY
  • Academy

When Steve left Apple in 1985, he targeted the educational market by creating NeXT. The computers weren’t a big hit and sales never took off.

The NeXT computer was considered expensive by academic advisors. At launch the equipment sold for $6,500 while educational institutions demanded prices between $2,000 and $3,000. Computer sales were far below forecasts and failure to market their products forced Jobs to shut down the company’s hardware business.

The operating system of the NeXT would, however, be responsible for the biggest turnaround in the lives of Jobs and Apple. The system had a graphical interface called NeXTSTEP and it quickly became famous for its friendly interface. Apple urgently needed a new Operating System and the option was to acquire NeXT and bring Jobs along.

The NeXT system replaced the obsolete Macintosh and served as the basis for Mac OS X. With this, Jobs returned to Apple as an advisor and the following year assumed the position of CEO.

Looking back NeXT was an astonishing success.
