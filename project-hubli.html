Hubli

Hubli was born out of the necessity of centralize and organize content to make teaching and learning collaborative for college students in a remote environment.

  • 2020

Methology

This project was conceived at Apple Developer Academy, by a group of five students. At the Academy we use the Challenge Based Learning (or CBL) which provides an efficient and effective framework for learning while solving real-world challenges.

The Challenge Learning Framework is divided into three interconnected phases: Engage, Investigate and Act. Each phase prepare you to move to the next phase. Within each of the phases there are opportunities for applying all sorts of methodologies and if necessary a return to an earlier phase.

Engage

In the first phase, we move from an abstract big idea to a concrete and actionable challenge

To find the most suitable theme that would engage the whole team, we went through a series of processes. And a series of virtual post-its. Seriously.

Investigate

Before creating this endless space fruity chaser, we started by gathering some references of casual games and graphic elements. By using forced connections, we were able to bring two unconventional topics together, such as Fruit and Space.

Before creating this endless space fruity chaser, we started by gathering some references of casual games and graphic elements. By using forced connections, we were able to bring two unconventional topics together, such as Fruit and Space.

The interface of the menu revolves around the concept of having the game just one play-tap away. To achieve that, it was designed to work as a layer that goes on top while the intergalactic spaceship is always traveling through the Juicy Way right behind it.

Safe Landing

In the first week, we developed the basic mechanics and submitted to Test Flight; in the second, we included monetization and analytics, and submitted for publication; on the third and final week the focus was on user acquisition, campaign development and metrics tracking.

Besides the assets and visual identity, I projected the menu and the HUD, and coded both interfaces using SwiftUI while André Tosin developed the mechanics using SpriteKit.
